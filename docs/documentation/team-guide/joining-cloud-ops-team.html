Joining the CloudOps team

Welcome to the team! Below is some information that you might find useful.

General information

  • Our standups are currently at 09.30am every morning.
  • Our sprints are two weeks and start on Wednesdays.
  • We plan our sprints through the GitHub extension Zenhub.

Team

Please see our team information for:

  • Who’s in the team
  • Our alliance
  • Our roadmap
  • Our ceremonies

Your first sprint ceremonies

When you join the team, we want you to feel welcome and included. With your input, we’ll collectively update our team alliance, and you’ll be able to meet the team if you haven’t done so already.

Tools

Communication

We use Microsoft Teams and Google Meet for team calls, and use Slack for direct calls.

Our main chat platform is Slack, and this is our preferred method of communication.

Download Slack from the Company Portal on your MoJ issued laptop.

We have three main Slack channels listed below.

#mojo-cloud-ops

The #mojo-cloud-ops channel is our team channel where we have team discussions about anything ranging from:

  • in-depth technical issues
  • our plans for the day
  • to request someone else from the team’s help
  • when we’re away for lunch
  • general musings

If you can’t make standup, post your standup update here.

#ask-cloud-ops

The #ask-cloud-ops channel is for anyone who isn’t in the team to:

  • ask questions about the services we run
  • get advice from the team

#mojo-cloud-ops-notifications

The #mojo-cloud-ops-notifications channel is where we direct notifications from GitHub, such as pull requests or issues.

It’s a noisy channel, so it’s up to you whether to join it.

GitHub teams to join (optional)

To add or close issues in our GitHub Project, you need to join the cloud-ops-admins team on GitHub. You can request to join or post a message in #mojo-cloud-ops channel and someone in the team will help you.

Learning and development

  • Pluralsight, ask for a pluralsight license in the #pluralsight Slack channel.

  • AWS Training. You can access the OGVA schedule via logging into here using you organisation email address. You can access the schedule directly here. For the courses, a reminder:

    • if you can no longer attend, please do withdraw from the course through your AWS training account - it will then become available to others
    • if your team has a need for training not listed here (or is fully booked), please do let me know and I can enquire about an MOJ-only course
    • if a class or course has a cost or isn’t part of the OGVA schedule, it is likely not part of the OGVA agreement

  • Learn Tech Trello Board for questions that you have about our Friday learning sessions

Further information

We’re currently building our documentation to cover core concepts and general practices within the team.

It’s worth having a read through the user guide and concepts pages.

There is a varying level of technical detail across the documentation, so if you have any questions or are interested in something in particular, feel free to reach out in #cloud-ops.
